Introduction¶

This project uses a dataset on medical appointment to investigate potential reasons for appointments that result in no-shows.

This data analysis uses a freely available dataset from Kaggle.com which features 100K+ medical appointments from the public healthcare system of the capital city of Espirito Santo State (Vitoria), Brazil. The dataset consists of 15 variables for each appoitnment including whether the patient showed up (or not) to the appointment.

This data analysis will conducted in a Jupyter Notebook using a Python 3 kernel.

The questions I will attempt to answer by analysing this dataset include:

  1. Are patients with a scholarship more or less likely to show up to an appointment?
  2. Does a patient's age influence the likelihood that they'll show up to an appointment?

Response to Question 1¶

The data indicates that there is a non-negligible difference in attendance between non-scholarship holders and scholarship holders. Indeed, the percentage of scholarship holders that did NOT attend their appointment was 24% while the percentage of non-scholarship holders that did NOT attend their appointment was 20%. Hence, there is a 4% increase in the No-Show Up rate for scholarship holders compared to non-scholarship holders patients.

Since scholarship holders are from low socio-economic background, their lower attendance rate may be linked to lack of access to transportation / reliable infrastructure and other related hardships associated with their status.

Response to Question 2¶

As the graph above shows, patients under 12 are the most likely to miss their appointments. This is probably due to their reliance on their parents to get to the doctor as well as the difficulties associated with travelling with young childer.
Older patients above 65 constitute are the most reliable patients with less than 2% of all appointments. Mid-life patients (25-44) are almost as likely to miss their appointments as younger children under 11.
